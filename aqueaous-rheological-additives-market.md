<p><p>The Aqueous Rheological Additives market consists of two main types: organic and inorganic rheological additives. Organic rheological additives, typically derived from natural sources or synthesized polymers, enhance the viscosity and stability of water-based formulations, making them ideal for paints and coatings. In contrast, inorganic rheological additives, often mineral-based, provide thixotropic and anti-settling properties, contributing to improved texture and application characteristics in products like adhesives and sealants. Both types play crucial roles in optimizing performance across various industries.</p></p>
