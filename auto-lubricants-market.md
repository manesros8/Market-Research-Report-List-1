<p><p>The auto lubricants market is primarily divided into two types: gasoline lubricants and diesel fuel lubricants. Gasoline lubricants are formulated to optimize performance in gasoline engines, enhancing fuel efficiency and reducing wear. They often contain additives to combat sludge and control engine temperature. In contrast, diesel fuel lubricants are designed for diesel engines, which operate under higher pressures and temperatures. These lubricants provide superior protection against soot and oxidation, ensuring longevity and reliability in diesel-powered vehicles.</p></p>
