<p><p>The refining market utilizes corrosion inhibitors to protect equipment and pipelines from degradation. Organic corrosion inhibitors are typically composed of carbon-based compounds, offering effective protection through adsorption on metal surfaces, and can include amines, esters, and other organic chemicals. In contrast, inorganic corrosion inhibitors, such as phosphates, silicates, and zinc-based compounds, provide protective films and neutralize corrosive agents through chemical reactions. Understanding these types helps in selecting suitable inhibitors to enhance the longevity and efficiency of refining processes.</p></p>
