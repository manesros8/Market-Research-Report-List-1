<p><p>The automotive brake booster market features several types, primarily categorized into single diaphragm and dual diaphragm boosters, with others including vacuum-assisted and hydraulic boosters. Single diaphragm boosters utilize one diaphragm to amplify brake pedal force, making them compact and lightweight. Dual diaphragm boosters employ two diaphragms for greater braking power and responsiveness, suitable for larger vehicles or performance applications. Other types incorporate different mechanisms, such as electric or pneumatic systems, catering to diverse automotive needs and enhancing safety and performance in braking systems.</p></p>
