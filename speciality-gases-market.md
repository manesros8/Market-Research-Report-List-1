<p><p>The specialty gases market is primarily divided into two types: pure gases and gas mixtures. Pure gases consist of single-component gases like helium, neon, and hydrogen, used in various applications such as electronics, healthcare, and research. Gas mixtures, on the other hand, are combinations of two or more gases tailored for specific applications, such as calibration gases for analytical instruments or specialty blends for welding. Both types play crucial roles in industries requiring precision and high-quality gases for various processes.</p></p>
