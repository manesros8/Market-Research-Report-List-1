<p><p>The starch-sugar enzymes market comprises several key types, including alpha amylase, beta amylase, and other enzymes. Alpha amylase is primarily responsible for breaking down complex carbohydrates into simpler sugars during the initial stages of starch processing. Beta amylase further hydrolyzes these starches into maltose and other sugars, enhancing sweetness and fermentation efficiency. Other enzymes in this market may include glucoamylase and pullulanase, which contribute to starch breakdown and sugar production in various industrial applications, such as food and biofuel.</p></p>
