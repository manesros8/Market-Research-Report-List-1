<p><p>The bacterial conjunctivitis drugs market is primarily segmented into three types: fluoroquinolones, aminoglycosides, and macrolides. </p><p>Fluoroquinolones are broad-spectrum antibiotics effective against a range of bacteria, commonly prescribed due to their potency and safety. Aminoglycosides are also used, particularly against gram-negative infections, but require careful monitoring due to potential side effects. Macrolides are known for their effectiveness against specific bacterial strains and are often preferred for patients with sensitivities or allergies to other antibiotic classes. Together, these categories address diverse treatment needs in managing bacterial conjunctivitis.</p></p>
