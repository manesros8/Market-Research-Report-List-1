<p><ul><li>Organic Rheology Modifiers</li><li>Inorganic Rheology Modifiers</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The rheology modifiers market is divided into two main types: organic and inorganic rheology modifiers. Organic rheology modifiers are typically carbon-based compounds, such as natural gums or synthetic polymers, which enhance the viscosity and stability of formulations in products like paints, coatings, and personal care items. In contrast, inorganic rheology modifiers include materials like clays and silica, which improve the flow and texture of products, often used in construction materials and industrial applications. Both types play critical roles in various industries.</p></p>
