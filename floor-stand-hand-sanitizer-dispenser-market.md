<p><p>The floor stand hand sanitizer dispenser market is segmented into two primary types: contact and non-contact dispensers. Contact dispensers require users to press a lever or button to dispense sanitizer manually, often increasing the potential for cross-contamination. In contrast, non-contact dispensers utilize sensors to automatically release sanitizer when hands are detected, promoting hygiene and reducing the spread of germs. As public health concerns rise, the non-contact category is gaining popularity due to its convenience and enhanced safety features.</p></p>
